- **Portfolio Size:** 5–8 high-conviction positions
- **Investment Horizon:** 2–4 years per position
- **Geographies:** India (NSE/BSE) and United States (NYSE/NASDAQ) only
- **Asset Class:** Listed equities only

## THE QGLP FRAMEWORK
Every stock must be evaluated across four dimensions in this order:

### 1. GROWTH (Primary Filter — must pass before anything else)
- Minimum earnings CAGR of **12–15%** over the 2–4 year horizon
- Growth must be driven by either:
  - TAM expanding at **2x+ GDP growth rate**, OR
  - Sustainable, documented market share gains
- Revenue growth must be credible and not dependent on one-off events

### 2. QUALITY
**Business Quality:**
- Durable competitive advantages (economic moats) — identify and name them
- Sustained ROE/ROCE **>15%** (sector-adjusted — see annexure)
- Strong and consistent free cash flow generation
- Favourable industry structure: duopoly or oligopoly preferred
- Fragmented or winner-take-all markets with uncertain outcomes are penalised

**Management Quality (automatic rejection if failed):**
- No ongoing governance controversies
- Promoter pledging **<25%**
- Clean regulatory background
- Capital allocation discipline — preference for organic growth and shareholder returns
- Meaningful promoter/insider alignment with shareholders
- Growth mindset evidenced by track record

### 3. LONGEVITY
Can this company maintain its competitive position and growth rate for **5+ years** beyond current valuations?

Evaluate:
- Historical ROE/ROCE consistency over 5+ years (rules out temporary winners)
- Market share trend stability — losing share for 2+ consecutive quarters is a red flag
- Management's track record of extending moats through innovation or strategy
- Regulatory or technological threats to the business model
- Succession planning depth and leadership bench strength

**Longevity Deterioration Triggers (immediate 25–50% position reduction):**
- Competitive position erosion for 2+ consecutive quarters without credible management response
- Growth decelerating below cost-of-capital threshold (>12% for growth companies; >8% for stable compounders)
- Management quality concerns: key departures, governance controversies, significant insider selling without buyback context
- Regulatory or technological threats to the core business model

**Longevity Bonus:** A company with 10+ years of proven competitive persistence receives favourable valuation treatment — PEG tolerance up to 1.4x.

### 4. PRICE
**Primary valuation metric: PEG Ratio**
PEG = Trailing P/E ÷ 3-year forward earnings CAGR

| PEG Level | Assessment |
|---|---|
| <1.0x | Exceptional value — strong buy signal |
| 1.0–1.2x | Reasonable — aligned with growth |
| 1.2–1.4x | Requires exceptional longevity evidence |
| >1.4x | Acceptable only in extraordinary cases with documented rationale |

**Valuation Triad (use the right metric for each business type):**
- P/E → Compounders with stable earnings
- EV/EBITDA → Cyclical businesses
- EV/Sales → Early-stage growth companies

**Hard Valuation Filters:**
- Debt/Equity **<1** (sector-adjusted — see annexure)
- Positive free cash flow
- PEG must be explicitly calculated and stated in every research report

## TRIM TRIGGERS (Valuation-Based)
- Valuations exceed **1–1.5 standard deviations above 5-year sector-adjusted averages** without fundamental upgrades
- PEG >1.4x companies: any growth deceleration toward cost-of-capital triggers immediate 15–20% trim
- Early-stage growth: EV/Sales in top quartile without margin expansion or revenue acceleration

## SECTOR-SPECIFIC ADJUSTMENTS

### FINANCIAL SERVICES
- Key metrics: NIM sustainability, CASA ratio, capital adequacy (CRR/SLR), NPA levels
- Growth filter: Loan book CAGR >12% in core segments with stable credit quality
- Quality: Top 3 market position preferred; technology infrastructure; deposit stickiness
- Longevity: Regulatory stability; CASA stability; ability to maintain NIM amid fintech disruption
- Red flag: Declining CASA for 3+ consecutive quarters

### PHARMACEUTICALS & BIOTECH
- Key metrics: Pipeline quality (minimum 5-year forward visibility), gross margins >50%, regulatory track record
- Growth filter: Earnings CAGR ≥12% from new molecules and emerging market expansion
- Quality: R&D productivity (approvals per $ invested); no repeated GMP violations; patent defensibility
- Red flag: Pipeline not sustaining growth beyond 3 years, or repeated approval rejections

### TECHNOLOGY & SAAS
- Key metrics: Rule of 40 (revenue growth + EBITDA margin ≥40), NRR >120%, CAC payback periods
- Growth filter: Revenue CAGR ≥12% with path to 20%+ within 2–4 year window
- Quality: Recurring revenue model; strong unit economics; competitive differentiation
- Red flag: Deteriorating NRR or new disruptive entrants without credible management response

### UTILITIES & INFRASTRUCTURE
- Calibrated growth expectations: 5–8% earnings growth acceptable
- Key metrics: Dividend sustainability, regulatory clarity, debt servicing capacity
- Longevity: Long-term power purchase agreements or tariff contracts
- Note: Smaller allocation weights — held for portfolio stability, not compounding

### CYCLICAL SECTORS (Metals, Autos, Chemicals)
- Context-dependent growth: 15%+ early-cycle; 8–10% mid-cycle acceptable
- Key metrics: EV/EBITDA vs historical averages; cycle positioning; balance sheet flexibility
- Exclusion: Pure cyclical plays (mining, legacy auto components) unless supported by secular tailwinds (EV, green energy, infrastructure)

### TELECOMS
- Debt/Equity threshold relaxed — leverage is structurally high
- Key metrics: ARPU growth, subscriber stability, market structure, debt coverage ratios
- Growth filter: 8–10% revenue CAGR with improving ARPU and subscriber economics
- Red flag: Subscriber losses or ARPU pressure for 2+ consecutive quarters

## GEOPOLITICAL & MACRO RISK PARAMETERS

### India-Specific Risks to Evaluate
- Regulatory risk: SEBI actions, sector-specific regulations
- Promoter governance risk (higher weight than in US context)
- INR currency risk for export-oriented businesses
- Dependency on government policy (PLI schemes, infrastructure spending)
- Competition from Chinese imports in manufacturing sectors

### US-Specific Risks to Evaluate
- China revenue concentration and export control exposure
- Federal Reserve interest rate sensitivity
- Regulatory risk: FTC/DOJ antitrust actions for large caps
- Customer concentration (any single customer >20% of revenue is a flag)

### Commodity Risks to Assess
- Input cost sensitivity: % of COGS exposed to key commodities
- Ability to pass through cost increases (pricing power test)
- Commodity-linked revenue concentration for upstream companies
